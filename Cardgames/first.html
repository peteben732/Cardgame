Some Introduction to Different Cardgames

  1. Bridge is a four-player, game divided into two phases: bidding and play. Teams compete to win tricks based on the strength of their hands and the bids they have made. Communication and strategy are key.

  2. Poker is a card game in which players place bets on who has the best hand according to variable rules depending on the type of poker you're playing.

  3. Blackjack is a classic casino game of luck and strategy. The goal of the game is to have your cards add up to 21 or as close to 21 as you can to beat the dealer.

  4. Spades is a casual card game developed in the 1930's in the USA. Spades is played with a basic set of 52 cards and card value ranks from 2, the lowest, to Ace, the highest.

  5. Kille, also called Harlequin, Cambio, Campio, Kambio or Kamfio, is a game played with special playing cards, dating from a mediaeval French gambling game. In Sweden, the game had its heyday during the 1750s, but it is one of the oldest card games still played.

  6. Vira is a trick-taking game. The actual trick play is preceded by an auction, as in Bridge. The player who bids the highest contract plays against the other two players.

  7. Hearts is a trick taking game where players try to go for the lowest score possible by avoiding any Hearts and especially the Queen of Spades.
